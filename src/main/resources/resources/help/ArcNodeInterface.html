Toolbar Buttons

Present Findings

One of the most important steps to making a diagnosis is identifying features that are present.


To indicate the presence of a finding:

  1. Left click the button.

  2. Left click the finding in the image to place an X indicating the location of the finding.

  3. Select the appropriate finding from the menu tree that appears.


To describe findings:

  1. Right click the finding node.

  2. Select “Further Specify” from the popup menu.

  3. Select the appropriate description from the menu tree that appears.


Note that the choices listed in the qualities popup menu are only the different qualities that particular finding can have in inflammatory diseases. The choices differ across findings.

Absent Findings

Sometimes the absence of a finding is just as important in making a diagnosis as present findings. Establishing absent findings could be very helpful when distinguishing between diseases that otherwise have the same findings.


To indicate that a finding is absent:

  1. Left click the button.

  2. Select the appropriate absent finding from the menu tree that appears.


Absent findings that you have identified display as yellow boxes, and say “NO” to distinguish them from present findings.

Hypotheses

As you are working through cases, it is useful to assert hypotheses that you are considering. A hypothesis is considered correct by the tutor as long as you have identified at least one feature that supports it.


To assert a hypothesis:

  1. Left click the button.

  2. Select the appropriate hypothesis from the menu tree that appears.

Drawing support links

Drawing support links between evidence and hypotheses is helpful in keeping your thoughts (and screen!) organized.


To draw a support link:

  1. Left click the button.

  2. Left press the mouse over the finding.

  3. Drag the mouse to the hypothesis. You will see a line being drawn.

  4. Release the mouse button.


An important thing to note about this program is that it allows users to hypothesize early on, and links can be drawn between findings that do not have any qualities added. If a quality is added later on that now makes the link between the finding and hypothesis incorrect, the user will be told that they have to reconsider that hypothesis.

Making a diagnosis

The ultimate goal of any case is to make a diagnosis. Diagnoses are different from hypotheses in that diagnoses must support ALL the features in the case. Therefore, hypotheses can be viewed as possible answers, while diagnoses are the final answer – the diagnosis or diagnoses that best fit all of the identified evidence. For some cases, the correct diagnosis is a differential diagnosis. The only choices provided are the hypotheses you have already asserted. You must assert at least one hypothesis before choosing a diagnosis.


To make a diagnosis:

  1. Left click the button.

  2. Select the appropriate diagnosis from the menu that appears.


Alternatively, you can make a hypothesis a diagnosis by holding down the left mouse button over the hypothesis, and dragging the hypothesis node down to the diagnosis portion of the screen.

Removing nodes from the screen

There are several ways to delete a node from the screen.


Either:

  • Right click the node and select remove from the menu.

  • Or, left click the node to select it and left click the button on the toolbar to remove the node from the screen. You can delete multiple items at a time by selecting multiple items and then clicking the button.

Done button

Once you are done working through a case, you can click the button. The tutor will ask if you are sure you want to submit the case. Once the case is submitted, the tutor will inform you that you are not done with a case if you have not worked through it entirely. If you have completed all for the case a large button will appear that says “Next Case”. Click the button to move on to the next case.


Menus

When locating findings and absent findings, describing qualities, and asserting hypotheses and diagnoses, options are selected from a menu tree that pops up in the test window.

Locating Items in the Menu Tree

There are two ways in which you can locate items in the menu trees: opening and closing the tree, or typing in a string to search for in the tree. To expand the tree, simply left click a “branch” of the tree to highlight it, and that branch will open up and display its contents. To open another branch, simply highlight that branch.


Alternatively, you can have the program search the tree for a particular string of letters by typing in a string and clicking the “Go” button. The tree will display any items containing that string. To go back to the original view of the tree, left click the “Reset” button.

Selecting an item from the menu tree

Either:

  • Click on the item to highlight it and click to add the selection to the selection list then press the OK button.

  • Right click the item to select it. The press the OK button.

  • Or, Double click the selection to select it. This will automatically close the menu window and assert your selection on the screen.


Even if the item appears to be highlighted from using the search tool, you still need to click the item to make sure it is selected.


You are not limited to choosing just one selection at a time. You can add multiple selections by selecting multiple things and pressing the OK button.

Deselecting an item from the selection list

To remove an item from the selection list:

  1. Click on the item to highlight it. Make sure you are in the selection list in the right portion of the menu tree screen.

  2. Click to remove the selection.

  3. Click OK.

Right Click Options

When you right click nodes, several options are available. These include Further Specify, Lookup Glossary, Show Location, and Remove.

Further Specify

The Further Specify option lets you add qualities to findings, or choose a more specific disease than what you originally selected. If you right click and select this option, the choices will be anything that is more specific than what you already selected. Simply click the appropriate item, and click the OK button.

Glossary

Glossary information is available about findings and diseases. When you select the Lookup Glossary option, a definition of the item will be given. For findings, an example picture of the finding will also be displayed.

Show Location

The Show Location option is available for present findings you have identified. This option will zoom the slide in to the location that you have placed your finding X. The X will also flash on the screen.

Remove

The Remove option is an alternative method of deleting items from the screen than using the button. Simply right click the item you wish to remove, and select the Remove option.


Frequently Asked Questions


How do I delete things?

You can delete findings, hypotheses, links, and diagnoses by right clicking the object you wish to delete and selecting “Remove Finding” from the choices listed.

How do I add quantities to findings?

The tutor allows you to describe the qualities of findings. Simply right click the finding box, select “Further Specify” from the popup menu, and go through the options and select the appropriate description.

What if I misplace my finding X? Can I move it?

If you place your finding X in the wrong part of the image, you have several options. You can cancel out of the action by clicking cancel in the popup menu window. Alternatively, you can still select your finding from the menu. The tutor will turn your X red and an alert message will appear. After closing the alert window, you can move the X by holding down the left mouse button and dragging the X to the correct location on the slide.

How do I rearrange the nodes on my screen?

At any time, you can move nodes around and reorganize your screen. Simply left press the mouse and drag nodes around as desired.

Why didn’t the tutor accept my support link?

For the tutor to accept links, you must be careful to begin and end your link when your mouse is inside the nodes. Additionally, your cursor needs to be inside the finding, not qualities.

Why aren’t there any choices to select when I click the diagnosis button?

When the diagnosis window appears, your choices will be all the hypotheses you have currently asserted. You must assert a hypothesis before you can make a diagnosis.
